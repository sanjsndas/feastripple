Cookie Policy

Last Updated: June 30, 2023

At FeastRipple, we use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience on our website. This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, the types of cookies we use, and how you can manage your cookie preferences. By continuing to use our website, you consent to our use of cookies in accordance with this policy.

Key Summary: Cookies are small text files that help websites remember your preferences. We use essential, performance, functional, and marketing cookies. You can control cookies through browser settings or our consent tool. Some features may not work without cookies. We respect Do Not Track signals and global privacy regulations.

1. What Are Cookies?

1.1 Definition and Function

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit websites. They store information about your interactions and preferences. Cookies enable websites to recognize users and remember important information. They make online experiences more efficient and personalized. Without cookies, websites would treat you as a new visitor each time you changed pages.

Cookies perform essential functions like maintaining secure sessions and remembering login details. They help websites understand user behavior through analytics. Cookies can store preferences like language settings or font sizes. They enable features like shopping carts on e-commerce sites. Most modern websites use multiple types of cookies for different purposes.

First-party cookies are set by the website you're visiting directly. Third-party cookies come from external services embedded in the site. Session cookies expire when you close your browser. Persistent cookies remain until their expiration date or until you delete them. Flash cookies and pixel tags are similar technologies with different storage mechanisms.

1.2 How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to provide basic website functionality and security. They enable features like account logins and shopping carts. Performance cookies help us understand how visitors use our site. This data informs improvements to navigation and content organization. Functional cookies remember your preferences for future visits.

Advertising cookies help deliver relevant marketing messages. They track campaign performance across different channels. Social media cookies enable sharing content and interacting with plugins. Analytics cookies collect aggregated usage statistics. We use this data to optimize page layouts and feature placement.

Some cookies help prevent fraudulent activity and enhance security. They can detect multiple failed login attempts. Load balancing cookies distribute server traffic efficiently. Preference cookies store settings like region or currency. We carefully evaluate each cookie's purpose and necessity before implementation.

1.3 Legal Basis for Cookie Usage

Essential cookies don't require consent as they're necessary for basic functions. For all other cookies, we obtain consent through our cookie banner. The legal basis is your explicit permission under privacy regulations. You can withdraw consent at any time through cookie settings. We document and store consent decisions for compliance purposes.

GDPR requires clear information about cookie purposes and durations. CCPA gives California residents rights to opt-out of cookie-based data sales. ePrivacy Directive mandates prior consent for non-essential cookies. We comply with all applicable regulations based on user location. Our cookie practices are regularly reviewed for legal compliance.

We conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments for cookie-related processing. Our Data Protection Officer oversees cookie compliance. Cookie policies are reviewed annually or when introducing new technologies. We maintain records of consent for regulatory audits. Users can access information about cookies used on their devices.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Essential Cookies

Essential cookies are strictly necessary for website operation. They enable core functions like security and authentication. Without these cookies, services you request cannot be provided. Examples include session cookies for logged-in users and load balancer cookies. These cookies don't store personally identifiable information.

Security cookies help prevent cross-site request forgery attacks. Authentication cookies maintain your logged-in status across pages. Shopping cart cookies remember items you've added during browsing. Fraud prevention cookies analyze patterns for suspicious activity. Consent management cookies store your cookie preferences.

Essential cookies typically expire when you close your browser. Some may persist longer for continuity of service. You can block these cookies through browser settings but site functionality will be impacted. We don't require consent for essential cookies under privacy laws. Their use represents our legitimate interest in website security.

2.2 Performance Cookies

Performance cookies collect information about how visitors use our site. This includes pages visited, time spent, and error messages received. The data is aggregated and anonymized for analysis. We use this information to improve website performance and user experience. These cookies don't collect information that identifies visitors.

Google Analytics cookies help measure traffic patterns and popular content. Heatmap cookies show where users click and scroll on pages. A/B testing cookies determine which page versions perform better. Error tracking cookies identify broken links or malfunctioning features. Load time cookies measure page speed across different regions.

Performance cookies may be first-party or third-party implemented. We configure analytics tools to respect Do Not Track signals. Data is aggregated before analysis to protect privacy. You can opt-out of performance cookies without affecting site functionality. These cookies typically persist for 2-24 months depending on purpose.

2.3 Functional Cookies

Functional cookies remember choices you make to enhance your experience. They enable personalized features and preferences. Examples include language selection, font size adjustments, and region-specific content. These cookies may also remember login details if you choose the "remember me" option. They make your interactions with the site more convenient.

Video player cookies store volume and playback speed preferences. Chat widget cookies maintain conversation history during browsing. Form autofill cookies remember information to save typing time. Accessibility cookies preserve high-contrast or text-to-speech settings. Survey cookies prevent showing the same questionnaire repeatedly.

The information these cookies collect may include personally identifiable data you've provided. They only track interactions with our website, not other sites. Functional cookies typically persist for several months to maintain preferences. You can disable them but may need to reset preferences each visit. Their use improves service quality without significant privacy impact.

2.4 Targeting/Advertising Cookies

Advertising cookies track browsing habits to deliver relevant ads. They remember sites you visit and interests you demonstrate. This information may be shared with third-party advertisers. These cookies help measure ad campaign effectiveness. They can make ads more useful and reduce repetition.

Retargeting cookies show our ads on other sites you visit. Affiliate cookies track referrals from partner websites. Social media cookies enable targeted advertising on platforms like Facebook. Frequency capping cookies limit how often you see the same ad. Conversion tracking cookies measure if ads lead to purchases.

These cookies are typically persistent and long-lasting. They may track you across multiple websites. We provide clear opt-out mechanisms for advertising cookies. You can manage preferences through our cookie consent tool. Disabling these cookies won't reduce ad quantity, just relevance.

2.5 Social Media Cookies

Social media cookies are set by services like Facebook and Twitter. They enable sharing content and interacting through plugins. These cookies may track your activity across multiple sites. Social platforms use them to build profiles of your interests. This data informs targeted advertising on their networks.

Like buttons use cookies to count shares and display your profile picture. Embedded videos set cookies to track viewing history. Comment widgets use cookies to identify logged-in users. Social login cookies enable authentication through platform accounts. Some cookies persist even when you're not actively using the plugins.

We implement social media widgets in privacy-enhanced modes where possible. You should review each platform's cookie policy for detailed information. Disabling these cookies may prevent some sharing features from working. Social cookies are controlled by the respective companies, not FeastRipple directly.

4. Managing Cookie Preferences

4.1 Browser Controls

Most browsers allow you to view and manage cookies through settings. You can typically find these under Privacy or Security sections. Options include accepting all cookies, blocking third-party cookies, or disabling cookies entirely. Browser settings also let you delete existing cookies. Some browsers offer per-site cookie controls for granular management.

Chrome users can access cookie settings via chrome://settings/cookies. Firefox provides detailed cookie management in Preferences > Privacy & Security. Safari allows cookie blocking through Preferences > Privacy. Edge offers cookie controls under Settings > Site Permissions. Opera includes cookie management in Settings > Privacy & Security.

Blocking all cookies may prevent some websites from working properly. Many sites require certain cookies for basic functionality. Browser settings apply globally to all websites you visit. Some browsers offer exceptions for specific trusted sites. Private/incognito modes automatically delete cookies when closed.

4.2 Our Consent Tool

Our cookie consent banner appears on your first visit. It explains cookie categories and requests your preferences. You can accept all, reject non-essential, or customize settings. The tool remembers your choices for future visits. You can reopen the consent dialog anytime through the footer link.

The tool provides detailed information about each cookie's purpose. Toggle switches let you enable/disable categories individually. Your selections take effect immediately without page reload. We store your preferences in a consent cookie (fr_consent). Changing preferences updates this cookie accordingly.

The tool complies with GDPR and CCPA requirements. It doesn't load non-essential cookies until consent is given. Withdrawal of consent stops further processing of affected cookies. Existing cookies remain until their expiration or manual deletion. The tool is regularly updated to reflect changes in our cookie usage.

4.3 Do Not Track Signals

Do Not Track (DNT) is a browser setting that signals your privacy preference. When enabled, your browser sends a DNT header with web requests. We honor DNT signals by disabling non-essential tracking cookies. Essential cookies required for site operation will still be set.

DNT doesn't automatically block all cookies or tracking technologies. Implementation varies across websites as no formal standard exists. We interpret DNT as opting out of third-party tracking cookies. First-party analytics may still collect aggregated usage data. The DNT setting doesn't affect cookie-based site functionality.

You can enable DNT in most browsers through privacy settings. Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Send a "Do Not Track" request. Firefox: Options > Privacy & Security > Send websites a "Do Not Track" signal. Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Ask websites not to track me. Edge: Settings > Privacy, search, and services > Send "Do Not Track" requests.

4.4 Opt-Out Tools

Industry programs offer centralized opt-out mechanisms for advertising cookies. The Digital Advertising Alliance provides YourAdChoices (optout.aboutads.info). Network Advertising Initiative offers an opt-out tool (optout.networkadvertising.org). These affect participating companies but not all advertisers.

Google provides an Analytics opt-out browser add-on. Facebook offers ad preference management in account settings. Many ad networks provide individual opt-out mechanisms. These tools typically work by setting an opt-out cookie on your device. Clearing cookies may require repeating the opt-out process.

For email tracking, disable automatic image loading in your email client. Use browser extensions that block tracking pixels. Consider privacy-focused browsers that limit tracking by default. Mobile devices offer advertising ID resets in system settings. VPNs and private browsing modes provide additional privacy layers.

4.5 Mobile Device Controls

Mobile operating systems provide cookie management through browser settings. iOS allows cookie blocking in Settings > Safari > Privacy & Security. Android offers controls in Chrome's Site Settings. Both platforms let you clear cookies and browsing data system-wide.

Mobile apps may use similar tracking technologies to cookies. iOS provides App Tracking Transparency framework for control. Android offers opt-out of ads personalization in Google settings. Reset your advertising ID periodically to disrupt tracking profiles. Review app permissions to limit unnecessary data access.

Tablet devices follow similar controls to their smartphone counterparts. Some mobile browsers offer enhanced privacy features by default. Consider using private browsing modes on shared devices. Be cautious when allowing "remember me" options on mobile browsers. Mobile operating systems are adding more granular privacy controls with each update.

5. Cookie-Related Technologies

5.1 Web Beacons/Pixel Tags

Web beacons are tiny transparent images embedded in web pages or emails. Also called pixel tags or clear GIFs, they track user interactions. When loaded, they can record page views, email opens, or ad impressions. Beacons often work with cookies to enhance tracking capabilities. They're commonly used in marketing campaigns and analytics.

We use pixels for conversion tracking and campaign measurement. They help determine if ads lead to desired actions. Email pixels track open rates to improve communication strategies. Some pixels facilitate retargeting across different websites. Unlike cookies, beacons can't be blocked through browser settings alone.

Blocking images in emails prevents open tracking. Browser extensions can detect and block tracking pixels. Privacy-focused email clients often disable remote images by default. Some analytics providers offer pixel-less alternatives using JavaScript. We minimize pixel use to essential marketing performance measurement.

5.2 Local Storage

Local Storage (HTML5 Web Storage) provides larger client-side storage than cookies. It persists after browser closure and has no expiration date. Data remains until explicitly cleared by user or website. We use localStorage for caching large preference sets and offline functionality. It's more efficient than cookies for storing non-sensitive data.

Common uses include saving complex UI states and application data. Local Storage can improve performance by reducing server requests. Unlike cookies, data isn't transmitted with each HTTP request. Storage limits are much higher (typically 5-10MB per domain). Data is only accessible to pages from the same origin.

You can clear Local Storage through browser developer tools. Some privacy extensions automatically purge storage data. Private browsing modes typically don't persist Local Storage. We only use it for legitimate functional purposes, not tracking. Data is encrypted when containing sensitive information.

5.3 IndexedDB

IndexedDB is a low-level API for client-side storage of significant amounts of structured data. It enables offline functionality for web applications. We use it to cache product catalogs and user-generated content. IndexedDB supports indexing for high-performance searches. It's more powerful than Local Storage for complex data needs.

Progressive Web Apps rely on IndexedDB for offline operation. It can store files and blobs in addition to structured data. Storage limits are typically 50% of available disk space. Data persists until explicitly cleared or the storage limit is reached. We implement automatic cleanup of stale IndexedDB data.

IndexedDB data is origin-scoped like Local Storage. Clearing site data through browser settings removes IndexedDB content. Some browsers allow inspecting stored data through developer tools. We encrypt sensitive information before storage. IndexedDB usage follows the same consent requirements as cookies.

5.4 Service Workers

Service Workers are scripts that run in the background, separate from web pages. They enable features like push notifications and offline caching. We use them to improve performance through resource caching. Service Workers can intercept network requests for advanced functionality. They're fundamental to Progressive Web App technology.

Cached assets reduce load times for return visitors. Offline mode provides basic functionality without internet access. Background sync allows deferred actions until connection resumes. Push notifications require explicit user permission. Service Workers have no access to DOM but can communicate with pages.

You can manage Service Workers through browser developer tools. Clearing site data typically unregisters Service Workers. Some browsers show active Service Workers in settings. We only register Service Workers for legitimate performance purposes. They operate independently of cookie consent but respect privacy preferences.

Service providers receiving cookie data are CCPA-compliant. We honor Global Privacy Control signals as opt-out requests. Cookie policies include CCPA-mandated disclosures. Staff training covers CCPA requirements for cookie usage. Compliance is verified through regular audits and assessments.

6.3 ePrivacy Directive

We provide detailed information about each cookie's purpose. Settings allow granular control by cookie category. Consent is renewed annually or when cookie usage changes significantly. The banner reappears if cookie preferences are reset. We maintain records of consent for regulatory compliance.
